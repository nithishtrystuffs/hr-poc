# Exit and Offboarding Policy

## Purpose and Scope
This policy outlines the process and entitlements applicable when an employee's employment with Nexora Technologies ends, whether through resignation, termination, or retirement. It applies to all full-time employees.

## Notice Period
Employees are required to serve a notice period of 60 days for resignation at the Manager level and above, and 30 days for all other full-time roles, as specified in the employment contract. Nexora Technologies may, at its discretion, offer to relieve an employee earlier or require payment in lieu of notice for the unserved portion, calculated on last drawn basic salary.

## Resignation Process
Resignations must be submitted in writing to the employee's manager and HR through the HR portal. Upon submission, HR initiates the offboarding workflow, which includes a formal acceptance of resignation, confirmation of the last working day, and scheduling of an exit interview. Employees should continue to perform their regular duties and complete a documented handover during the notice period.

## Exit Interview
An exit interview is conducted by HR, typically within the final week of the notice period, either in person or virtually. The exit interview covers reasons for leaving, feedback on the employee's experience, and confirmation that all company property will be returned. Feedback from exit interviews is kept confidential and used in aggregate for retention analysis; it is not shared with the employee's direct manager unless the employee consents.

## Asset and Access Return
All company assets, including laptops, access cards, mobile devices, and any other equipment, must be returned to IT or Admin on or before the last working day. System access, including email, VPN, and internal applications, is deactivated at end of day on the last working day unless a documented business need requires a short extension, which must be approved by both HR and IT Security. Employees who fail to return assets may have the value deducted from final settlement.

## Final Settlement
Final settlement, including any pending salary, encashment of unused annual leave (up to the carry-forward cap defined in the Leave Policy), and any applicable bonus or reimbursements, is processed within 45 days of the last working day. The final settlement statement is shared with the employee via the HR portal, along with Form 16 and other statutory documents as applicable.

## Non-Disclosure and Post-Employment Obligations
The confidentiality obligations outlined in the Code of Conduct and the employee's signed NDA continue to apply after the last working day. Departing employees are reminded of these obligations during the exit interview and are required to acknowledge them in writing as part of the offboarding checklist. Any non-compete or non-solicitation clauses in the employment contract remain enforceable per their stated terms.

## Involuntary Termination
In cases of termination for performance or conduct reasons, the offboarding process follows the same asset return and access deactivation steps, but notice period and severance terms are governed by the specific circumstances and applicable employment law rather than the standard resignation notice period. Employees terminated for cause forfeit eligibility for rehire consideration.

## Rehire Eligibility
Employees who resign in good standing, complete their full notice period, and receive a "Meets Expectations" or higher final review are generally eligible for rehire consideration, subject to a minimum gap of 6 months and standard reapplication through the normal hiring process. HR maintains rehire eligibility status in the employee's record for reference in future applications.

## Reference Checks
Nexora Technologies provides employment verification (dates of employment, role, and, where authorized by the departing employee, salary) to prospective employers through HR. Managers are not authorized to provide informal references on behalf of the company; all reference requests should be directed to HR.
