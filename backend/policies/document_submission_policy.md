# Document Submission Policy

## Purpose

Employees must submit all mandatory documents during onboarding to complete
verification and begin the onboarding process. Certain documents must also
be returned, confirmed, or reissued during offboarding. This policy defines
which documents are required at each stage, who must submit them, and what
happens if documents are missing, incomplete, or delayed.

---

## Required Documents for Freshers

| Document          | Mandatory | Purpose                           |
| ----------------- | --------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Resume            | ✅        | Employee profile verification      |
| Offer Letter      | ✅        | Employment confirmation            |
| SSN Scanned Copy  | ✅        | Identity and payroll verification  |
| Bank Details      | ✅        | Salary processing                  |

---

## Required Documents for Experienced Employees

| Document          | Mandatory | Purpose                                    |
| ----------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Resume            | ✅        | Employment record                           |
| Offer Letter      | ✅        | Employment confirmation                     |
| SSN Scanned Copy  | ✅        | Identity verification                       |
| Bank Details      | ✅        | Salary processing                           |
| Relieving Letter  | ✅        | Proof of separation from previous employer  |

An employee is considered "experienced" if they have prior full-time work
history with another employer. Freshers are employees joining their first
full-time role and do not have a Relieving Letter to submit.

---

## Optional Documents

- Passport
- Visa documents (if applicable)
- Professional certifications

Optional documents are not required to complete onboarding but may be
requested for specific roles, immigration status, or client requirements.

---

## Missing Documents

If a required document is missing:

- Employee status remains **Pending Validation**
- HR is notified automatically
- The employee can upload the missing document at a later time
- Onboarding continues only after all mandatory documents are received and
  verified

Employees in Pending Validation status may face delays in system access,
payroll setup, or final onboarding sign-off until the outstanding documents
are submitted.

---

## Verification Process

Once a document is uploaded, HR verifies:

- Document clarity (the file is legible and not corrupted)
- Correct employee information (name, ID, and details match employee records)
- Completeness (all pages or required fields are present)
- Validity (the document is current and not expired, where applicable)

If verification fails, HR rejects the document and requests a new upload
from the employee. The employee is notified of the reason for rejection so
they can correct and resubmit.

---

## Documents at Offboarding

Document requirements don't end at onboarding. When an employee exits, the
following document-related steps apply, in line with the Exit and
Offboarding Policy:

- **Relieving Letter (issued to the employee):** Generated by HR after the
  employee completes their notice period, returns company assets, and clears
  the exit checklist. Typically issued on or shortly after the last working
  day.
- **Final settlement statement:** Shared via the HR portal within 45 days of
  the last working day.
- **Statutory and tax documents:** Issued along with the final settlement,
  as applicable.
- **Experience/service certificate:** Available on request from HR after the
  relieving letter is issued, confirming role, tenure, and last working day.
- **NDA and offboarding acknowledgment:** The employee is required to
  re-acknowledge confidentiality and post-employment obligations in writing
  as part of the offboarding checklist.

No new onboarding-style documents (resume, SSN, bank details, etc.) need to
be resubmitted at offboarding — those remain on file from onboarding unless
HR specifically requests an update (for example, updated bank details for
final settlement).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Onboarding — Freshers

**What documents do I need to submit as a new joiner?**
As a fresher, you need to submit:
- Resume
- Offer Letter
- SSN Scanned Copy
- Bank Details

**Do I need a relieving letter?**
No. Relieving letters are only required for experienced employees who have
worked at a previous employer.

**I don't have a previous employer. What do I submit instead?**
Nothing in place of a Relieving Letter — freshers are only required to
submit Resume, Offer Letter, SSN Scanned Copy, and Bank Details.

### Onboarding — Experienced Employees

**I have prior work experience. What documents do I need?**
You must submit:
- Resume
- Offer Letter
- SSN Scanned Copy
- Bank Details
- Relieving Letter

**I haven't received my relieving letter from my previous employer yet. Can I still join?**
Yes, you can begin onboarding with your other documents, but your status
will remain Pending Validation until the Relieving Letter is submitted.
Please inform HR of the expected delay so they can follow up with you.

**My previous company calls it a "experience letter," not a "relieving letter." Is that the same thing?**
It depends on what the document confirms. If it confirms your last working
day and separation from your previous employer, HR can typically accept it
in place of a Relieving Letter — check with HR to confirm before your
onboarding deadline.

### Onboarding — General

**Can I join without submitting my bank details?**
No. Bank details are required before payroll can be processed. Salary
disbursal cannot begin without verified bank information on file.

**Can I upload documents later?**
Yes. However, onboarding remains in the Pending Validation stage until all
mandatory documents are submitted and verified by HR.

**Why do I need to submit my SSN?**
The SSN scanned copy is used for identity verification, payroll processing,
and legal/compliance purposes. It is handled confidentially and accessible
only to authorized HR personnel.

**What happens if my document is rejected during verification?**
HR will notify you of the reason for rejection (for example, blurry scan,
mismatched details, or expired document) and ask you to upload a corrected
version. Onboarding remains in Pending Validation until a valid document is
received.

**Is a passport required?**
No. A passport is an optional document and is only required for employees
who need it for travel, immigration, or specific client engagements.

**How long does document verification take?**
Verification is typically completed within a few business days of upload.
If it's taking longer, check your status on the HR portal or reach out to
HR directly.

**Is there a deadline to submit my onboarding documents?**
Mandatory documents should be submitted as early as possible during
onboarding. Delays affect your Pending Validation status and can push back
system access, payroll setup, and final onboarding sign-off.

### Offboarding — Documents

**When will I receive my relieving letter?**
Your relieving letter is issued by HR after you complete your notice
period, return all company assets, and clear the exit checklist —
typically on or shortly after your last working day.

**What if I need my relieving letter urgently, for example for a new employer?**
Contact HR directly. The letter is generally only issued once offboarding
formalities (asset return, access deactivation, exit interview) are
complete, so timing depends on how quickly those steps are finished.

**Do I need to resubmit any documents when I resign or leave?**
No. Documents submitted during onboarding remain on file. You may only need
to update your bank details if they've changed, so your final settlement
is processed correctly.

**Will I get a certificate confirming my employment, separate from the relieving letter?**
Yes. An experience or service certificate confirming your role, tenure, and
last working day is available on request from HR after your relieving
letter has been issued.

**How do I get proof of employment for a background check after I leave?**
Reference and employment verification requests are handled by HR, not by
individual managers. Prospective employers should direct verification
requests to HR.

**Where can I find my final settlement and tax documents?**
These are shared through the HR portal within 45 days of your last working
day, along with any applicable statutory documents.
