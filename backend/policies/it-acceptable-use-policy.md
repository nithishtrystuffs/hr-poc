# IT Acceptable Use and Data Security Policy

## Purpose and Scope
This policy governs the acceptable use of Nexora Technologies' IT systems, devices, and data by all employees, contractors, and interns. It applies to company-owned devices as well as any personal device used to access company systems or data.

## Account and Password Security
Employees must use a unique, strong password for their Nexora account, meeting a minimum of 12 characters with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Passwords must not be shared with anyone, including managers or IT staff. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all systems that support it, including email, VPN, and the HR portal. Passwords must be changed immediately if an employee suspects their account has been compromised, and reported to IT Security within 1 hour of discovery.

## Company Device Usage
Company-issued laptops and devices remain the property of Nexora Technologies and must be used primarily for business purposes. Employees must not install unauthorized software, disable antivirus or endpoint protection tools, or attempt to bypass IT-managed security configurations. Devices must be locked whenever unattended, even briefly, using the screen lock shortcut.

## Data Classification and Handling
Company data is classified into three tiers: Public (safe for external sharing), Internal (for employee use only, e.g. internal wikis, org charts), and Confidential (client data, financial records, source code, employee personal data). Confidential data must never be shared via personal email, personal cloud storage, or unapproved messaging apps. Confidential data may only be stored on approved company systems (company Google Drive, internal servers) and must be encrypted when transmitted externally, with IT Security approval required for any external sharing.

## Email and Communication Security
Employees must not open attachments or click links from unknown or suspicious senders. Suspected phishing emails should be reported using the "Report Phishing" button in the email client and forwarded to security@nexoratech.example without further interaction. Employees must not use personal email accounts to conduct company business or forward company documents to personal accounts.

## Software and Application Approval
Only software approved by IT and listed in the internal Software Catalog may be installed on company devices. Employees requiring a new tool must submit a request through the IT portal, which is reviewed for security and licensing compliance, typically within 3 business days. Use of unapproved AI tools or browser extensions to process confidential company or client data is strictly prohibited.

## Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
Personal devices used to access company email or the HR portal must have a passcode or biometric lock enabled and must be enrolled in the company's Mobile Device Management (MDM) system, which allows IT to remotely wipe company data (not personal data) if the device is lost, stolen, or the employee exits the company. Employees who decline MDM enrollment will not be permitted to access company systems from personal devices.

## Incident Reporting
Any suspected security incident, including lost or stolen devices, suspected malware, unauthorized access, or accidental data exposure, must be reported to IT Security immediately, and no later than 2 hours after discovery, via the emergency IT hotline listed on the intranet. Delayed reporting may worsen the impact of a breach and is treated as a separate policy concern from the incident itself.

## Remote Access and VPN
All access to internal company systems from outside the office network must go through the company VPN. Split-tunneling (routing only some traffic through the VPN) is disabled by default and may only be enabled with IT Security approval for specific, documented business needs.

## Data Retention and Disposal
Confidential data must not be retained longer than necessary for its business purpose, per the retention schedules published by each department. When company devices are retired or reassigned, they must be returned to IT for secure wiping; employees must not attempt to wipe or dispose of devices themselves.

## Consequences of Violation
Violations of this policy, including sharing confidential data through unapproved channels, disabling security controls, or failing to report an incident promptly, are treated as serious matters and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, in line with the Code of Conduct's disciplinary process.
