# Code of Conduct

## Purpose and Scope
This Code of Conduct sets out the standards of behavior expected of every employee, contractor, and intern at Nexora Technologies. It applies at all company premises, company-sponsored events, and in any professional interaction representing the company, including remote work and online communication.

## Professional Behavior
Employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, vendors, and partners with respect and courtesy at all times. Disruptive behavior, shouting, public humiliation of colleagues, or deliberately undermining a colleague's work are considered violations of this policy and may result in disciplinary action ranging from a formal warning to termination, depending on severity and recurrence.

## Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination
Nexora Technologies has zero tolerance for harassment or discrimination based on race, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, age, disability, marital status, or any other legally protected characteristic. This includes verbal, physical, or visual conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment. All employees, regardless of level, must complete annual anti-harassment training. Complaints are handled under the separate Anti-Harassment Complaint Procedure, which guarantees confidentiality and protection from retaliation for anyone raising a concern in good faith.

## Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose to HR any situation where personal interests could conflict with the interests of Nexora Technologies, including financial interests in a competitor, vendor, or client, or a close personal relationship with someone they directly manage or are managed by. Disclosures are reviewed by HR and, where necessary, managed through role changes or recusal from relevant decisions. Failure to disclose a known conflict of interest is treated as a serious policy violation.

## Gifts and Entertainment
Employees may not accept gifts, hospitality, or entertainment from clients, vendors, or partners with a value exceeding INR 5,000 without prior disclosure to their manager and HR. Cash or cash-equivalent gifts of any value must be declined. This restriction exists to prevent situations where a business decision could be perceived as influenced by personal benefit.

## Confidentiality
All employees are required to protect confidential company information, including client data, financial information, product roadmaps, and internal communications, both during and after their employment. Confidential information must not be shared with anyone outside the company, or with colleagues who do not have a legitimate business need to know, without appropriate authorization. This obligation continues after an employee's exit and is reinforced through the exit NDA reminder process.

## Use of Company Resources
Company equipment, software licenses, and facilities are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use of company equipment (e.g. checking personal email) is permitted provided it does not interfere with work, incur significant cost, or violate any other company policy, including the IT Acceptable Use Policy.

## Substance-Free Workplace
Employees must not report to work, or be present at company premises or company events, under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs. Consumption of alcohol at company-sponsored events is permitted in moderation for employees of legal drinking age, where explicitly authorized as part of the event.

## Reporting Violations
Employees who witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct should report it to their manager, HR, or through the anonymous ethics hotline listed on the HR portal. Reports are investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is itself a serious violation of this policy and will be treated accordingly.

## Disciplinary Process
Violations of this Code of Conduct are addressed through a progressive disciplinary process: verbal warning, written warning, final written warning, and termination, depending on severity. Serious violations, including harassment, discrimination, fraud, or breach of confidentiality, may result in immediate termination without progressing through earlier stages.
